Molding Leaders in Tech: My Experience Guiding Junior Developers

Shaping the leaders of tomorrow is a responsibility that comes with its own unique set of challenges. Stepping into a leadership role in the tech industry is a blend of mentorship, management, and constant learning. My journey from a technical role as a CTO to a guide for budding tech enthusiasts has been a fascinating one, packed with learning experiences and rewarding outcomes.


In my time as the CTO of Eflyn, I developed the entire next-generation Kiosk Platform from scratch. This experience armed me with invaluable expertise in various aspects of software development. But, transitioning from this role to a leadership one was like stepping into a new world.


"Leadership is not about being in charge. It's about taking care of those in your charge."

My role as a guide began with sharing my knowledge, helping junior developers understand the nuances of coding, testing, and debugging. But, I soon realized that true leadership went beyond sharing knowledge. It involved instilling a sense of responsibility, fostering a growth mindset, and nurturing the creative sparks in each individual.


I made it my mission to provide junior developers with the tools and training needed to assume responsibilities for various components and services in our architecture. Through one-on-one coaching sessions, code reviews, and constructive feedback, I was able to foster a culture of continuous learning and improvement.


In this journey of molding leaders in tech, there were highs and lows, successes and setbacks. But, the sight of my mentees confidently navigating the tech landscape, solving complex problems, and continually striving for excellence made it all worthwhile.


Being a leader in tech is not just about having a command over technology. It's about cultivating a supportive environment that motivates, inspires, and drives people to push their limits and achieve their full potential.
